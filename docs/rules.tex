\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Subspace Lattice is a two-player strategy game on an $11\times 11$ sector grid.
It combines individual piece movement with territory projected by a
\textbf{Sensor Net}. Online play and local AI soft-ship
\textbf{hybrid-fleet} (Integration Hold, Contested Space, late activation,
Initiative Relay). Legacy \textbf{hybrid} (instant sector win, mirrored
setup) and \textbf{classic} (chess-like, no Sensor Net movement effects)
remain for sims and regression. This document describes Sensor Net play;
fleet knobs are in Section~\ref{sec:clock}.

Digital skill uses the same \textbf{TEI} (Tactical Efficiency Index) as
Warp~12 (Section~\ref{sec:tei}) across the IWGF product family.
\end{abstract}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Players and turn order}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Two players, \textbf{White} and \textbf{Black}, each command a fleet.
White moves first. Players alternate turns. On a turn, a player must move
exactly one of their pieces according to the movement rules in
Section~\ref{sec:movement}.

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{The board}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item The board is an $11\times 11$ grid of \emph{coordinates}
        $(x,y)$ with $0 \le x,y \le 10$.
  \item The center cell $(5,5)$ is a \textbf{Gravity Well}.
  \item Gravity Wells \textbf{block}: pieces may not occupy them or path
        through them. They have no other geometric effects.
\end{itemize}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{The fleet}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Each side begins with eight pieces:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Piece} & \textbf{Count} & \textbf{Symbols (White / Black)} \\
\midrule
Command Hub   & 1 & $\mathrm{K}$ / king-style hub \\
Escort        & 3 & pawn-style \\
Infiltrator   & 2 & knight-style \\
Beam          & 2 & rook-style \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

White deploys along the low-$y$ edge (near $y=0$); Black mirrors on the
far rank ($y=10$). Exact starting coordinates match the engine setup.
Under \texttt{hybrid-fleet}, White also receives Initiative Relay Escorts
(Section~\ref{sec:clock}).

\rulesfig{opening-hybrid}{%
  Opening setup under \texttt{hybrid}: mirrored fleets, no Initiative Relay.
  White along $y=0$ (bottom of the capture); Gravity Well at $(5,5)$.}

\rulesfig{opening-fleet-relay}{%
  Soft-ship \texttt{hybrid-fleet} opening. Highlighted Escort at $(5,3)$ is
  White's Initiative Relay---forward compensation for the first move.}

Capturing an enemy piece is done by landing on its coordinate (subject to
legal movement). The captured piece is removed from the board.

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Sensor Net (Sovereign Space)}
\label{sec:sensor-net}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Distance on the Sensor Net uses \textbf{Chebyshev} (king-move) metric:
\[
  d\bigl((x_1,y_1),(x_2,y_2)\bigr)
  = \max\bigl(|x_1-x_2|,\,|y_1-y_2|\bigr).
\]

\subsection{Radiation}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{0.55\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Source} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
Command Hub
  & Always radiates radius $R_{\mathrm{hub}}=3$. \\
Escort
  & Radiates radius $R_{\mathrm{escort}}=1$ only if \emph{linked}
    to its Command Hub (see below). \\
Beam / Infiltrator
  & Do not radiate. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A player's \textbf{Sensor Net} (also called \textbf{Sovereign Space}) is the
set of all coordinates within any of that player's radiating radii.
Occupied squares are included. Gravity Wells may fall inside a radius but
are excluded from Sector Integration counting (Section~\ref{sec:victory}).

\subsection{Linking}

Friendly pieces form a link graph: two friendly pieces are connected if
their Chebyshev distance is at most $L=2$ (\texttt{linkDistance}).

Start a breadth-first search from the Command Hub through that graph.
Every Escort in the connected component is \textbf{linked} and radiates.
Escorts outside the component do not expand the net.

\rulesfig{sensor-net-sovereign}{%
  Linked White Escorts projecting Sensor Net / Sovereign Space (blue tint).
  Coverage follows the Hub through the escort chain.}

\rulesfig{broken-escort}{%
  Broken relay: the Escort at $(8,1)$ is more than two squares from the linked
  chain ending at $(5,1)$, so it is dark and does not radiate. Closing the gap
  restores coverage.}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Target Lock}
\label{sec:detection}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

A piece is \textbf{Target Locked} (engine term: \texttt{detected}) if its
current coordinate lies in the \emph{opponent's} Sensor Net.

While Target Locked, the piece loses special movement and may only move
\textbf{one orthogonal step} (up, down, left, or right), regardless of type.

\rulesfig{target-lock}{%
  White Infiltrator (highlighted) sits inside Black's Sensor Net and is
  Target Locked: warp is suppressed; only one orthogonal step is legal.}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Movement}
\label{sec:movement}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Unless Target Locked (Section~\ref{sec:detection}), pieces move as follows.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.22\textwidth}p{0.35\textwidth}p{0.28\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Piece} & \textbf{Normal} & \textbf{If Target Locked} \\
\midrule
Command Hub
  & One step in any of the eight directions
  & One orthogonal step \\
Escort
  & One orthogonal step
  & One orthogonal step \\
Infiltrator
  & \textbf{Warp} to any coordinate that is empty or enemy-occupied
    and \emph{not} inside the enemy Sensor Net.
    Path is ignored (may jump).
    Under \texttt{hybrid-spool}, warps use Navigational Target Lock
    (Section~\ref{sec:spool}).
  & One orthogonal step \\
Beam
  & Orthogonal rook slide: clear path (no pieces or Gravity Wells
    between). \emph{Every} intermediate square and the destination
    must lie in the \textbf{own} Sensor Net.
  & One orthogonal step \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A piece may not move onto a friendly piece or a Gravity Well.
Moving onto an enemy piece captures it.

\rulesfig{beam-lane}{%
  Beam lane: highlighted path must stay inside White's own Sensor Net.
  Beams cannot slide through empty space outside coverage.}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Navigational Target Lock (\texttt{hybrid-spool})}
\label{sec:spool}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Experimental rules version sharing hybrid Sensor Net defaults.
An Infiltrator warp requires two turns:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Spool.} Announce a currently legal warp destination.
        The piece does not move. The turn ends.
  \item \textbf{Execute.} On a later turn of yours, the only legal action
        for that Infiltrator is the locked coordinate. If it remains legal,
        the warp (and any capture) resolves. If it has become illegal
        (e.g.\ swallowed by the enemy Sensor Net), the jump fails: the
        Infiltrator stays put and the turn is still consumed.
\end{enumerate}

%% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Victory}
\label{sec:victory}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

The game ends when any of the following occurs after a move:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Surgical Strike.}
        Capture the enemy Command Hub. The capturer wins.
  \item \textbf{Sector Integration.}
        After your move, your Sensor Net covers at least
        $\rho = 0.45$ (45\%) of all non--Gravity-Well coordinates.
        You win immediately.
        Let $N$ be the number of non-well cells and $C$ the number of
        those cells in your Sensor Net; you win if $C/N \ge \rho$.
  \item \textbf{No legal moves.}
        The opponent has no legal moves. You win.
\end{enumerate}

\rulesfig{surgical-strike}{%
  Surgical Strike threat: White Escort adjacent to the Black Command Hub.
  Capturing the Hub ends the battle immediately.}

\paragraph{Design intent (not a separate win rule).}
Surgical Strike is the primary tactical resolution.
Sector Integration is a \textbf{forcing clock}: it rewards sustained net
expansion and punishes endless turtling, but it is not meant to be a
fifty--fifty coin-flip with hub hunting. Simulation scorecards prefer
configurations where Sector Integration accounts for roughly
$25$--$40\%$ of decided games. The clock's health is verified by
\emph{ablation}: with the sector path disabled under paired seeds,
self-play must degrade measurably (more deadlocks, longer grinds) —
see \texttt{docs/adr/005-functional-clock-gate.md}. Notably, working
clock pressure mostly cashes out as \emph{faster hub captures}, so
sector wins need not be the slowest win type. Defaults change only
through the human gate (Section~\ref{sec:params}).

\section{Fleet soft-ship (\texttt{hybrid-fleet})}
\label{sec:clock}
\label{sec:fleet-preset}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Online rooms and local AI soft-ship \texttt{hybrid-fleet}. The mechanics
below remain gated by human playtest
(\texttt{docs/playtest-fleet-checklist.md}) before treating them as the
sole ``official hybrid'' in marketing copy. Track~A production sims
(2026-07-21) passed fairness under
$\rho=0.45$ / hold $1$ / contested / activation $100$ / Initiative Relay $1$
(White $45\%$ / Black $55\%$, sector $30\%$, OpenSkill sep $13.3$).

Legacy \texttt{hybrid} keeps instant sector wins and a mirrored setup.

\begin{description}[leftmargin=*]
  \item[Integration Hold (\texttt{sectorHoldPlies} $=K$).]
        Sector Integration no longer wins instantly. Coverage
        $\ge \rho$ must persist for $K$ consecutive plies (either
        side's). Each side has a streak counter that ticks on every ply
        its coverage holds and resets to zero when coverage drops below
        $\rho$; a side wins when its counter reaches $K$, so the
        opponent always has $K-1$ plies to break the net. If both
        counters reach $K$ together, neither wins while the tie persists;
        this prevents the activation ply from granting a hidden color
        advantage. The clock resolves when only one side still meets its hold.
        $K=0$ restores the instant win.
  \item[Contested Space (\texttt{contestedCellsNeutral}).]
        A coordinate covered by \emph{both} Sensor Nets counts for
        \emph{neither} side's Sector Integration coverage. Projecting
        your net into the opponent's territory directly stalls their
        clock (and resets their Integration Hold streak if it drops
        them below $\rho$).
\end{description}

\rulesfig{contested-space}{%
  Contested Space: where White and Black nets overlap (purple), cells count
  for neither side's Sector Integration coverage.}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=*]
  \item[Late-game activation (\texttt{sectorActivationPly} $=A$).]
        The Sector Integration clock is \textbf{disarmed} before $A$
        completed plies: no sector wins occur and Integration Hold
        streaks do not accrue. From ply $A$ onward, normal
        coverage/hold rules apply. $A=0$ arms the clock from the start.
  \item[Initiative Relay (\texttt{firstPlayerRelayCount} $=N$).]
        The first player begins with $N$ additional Escorts placed
        forward of the opening formation and linked through the central
        Escort. This is a visible seat compensation for the structural
        first-mover disadvantage observed under equal-strength play.
        $N=0$ restores the mirrored setup.
\end{description}

\rulesfig{objective-hud}{%
  Objective HUD with the Sector Integration clock armed (ply past
  activation). Coverage bars show each fleet's progress toward $\rho$.}

\section{Classic mode (reference)}
\label{sec:classic}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\texttt{classic} ignores Sensor Net for movement and Sector Integration.
Movement is chess-inspired:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Command Hub: one step any direction
  \item Escort: one orthogonal step
  \item Infiltrator: knight leap ($2{+}1$)
  \item Beam: unrestricted orthogonal rook slide (blocked by pieces/wells)
\end{itemize}

Wins: Surgical Strike or no legal moves only.

\section{TEI (Tactical Efficiency Index)}
\label{sec:tei}
%% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Subspace Lattice uses the same TEI presentation layer as Warp~12 so a
grade like \texttt{V67} means the same thing across IWGF products.
The normative implementation is imported from \texttt{warp12-engine}
(see \texttt{docs/adr/003-tei-grades.md}).

\subsection{What TEI is}

Backend ratings are OpenSkill Bayesian skill estimates $(\mu,\sigma)$.
TEI is \textbf{display-only}: it never replaces the stored $(\mu,\sigma)$.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{0.62\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Term} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\textbf{TEI}
  & Grade string: letter + score, e.g.\ \texttt{V67}. \\
\textbf{TEI Grade} (letter)
  & Confidence from $\sigma$:
    \textbf{E} Elite, \textbf{V} Veteran, \textbf{C} Consistent,
    \textbf{I} Improving, \textbf{P} Provisional. \\
\textbf{TEI Score} (number)
  & Integer $0$--$99$ from the conservative skill estimate
    $\mu - 3\sigma$, normalized on a shared population scale
    ($\mathrm{min}\,\mu=10$, $\mathrm{max}\,\mu=50$). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Letter transitions use \textbf{hysteresis} so small $\sigma$ noise does
not flicker the grade every match. New or uncertain ratings sit at
\textbf{P}; more games tighten $\sigma$ and improve the letter.

\subsection{AI strength anchors}

Local AI rated games update the human against fixed OpenSkill \textbf{anchors}
(Warp points-track). UI strengths map as follows (display TEI at the
anchor itself):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lllll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{UI} & \textbf{Search} & \textbf{Track} & $(\mu,\sigma)$ & \textbf{Anchor TEI} \\
\midrule
Fast & Heuristic & Ensign & $(13.5,\,4.0)$ & \texttt{P0} \\
Normal & MCTS $50$ & Lieutenant & $(24.5,\,3.5)$ & \texttt{I10} \\
Strong & MCTS $200$ & Commander & $(40.0,\,3.0)$ & \texttt{I52} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

These anchors are Lattice-calibrated (2026-07-21 hybrid-fleet ladder:
\texttt{docs/sim-runs/evolve-20260721-ai-tier-calibration.jsonl}).
$\mu$ comes from measured self-play; $\sigma$ is a tight known-opponent
value so the TEI letter is not stuck provisional. Re-run
\texttt{yarn calibrate:ai} after changing search budgets or shipping rules.

\subsection{Federation commission (flavor)}

Warp maps TEI strings to a naval commission ladder (Cadet through Fleet
Admiral). Subspace Lattice may show the same commission flavor over TEI
when the UI surfaces ranks; commission is \textbf{not} a second rating
system and does not change update math.

\subsection{Scope in this product}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Simulations / ladders:} TEI is computed for agent ratings.
  \item \textbf{Local AI:} Fast / Normal / Strong map to Ensign /
        Lieutenant / Commander anchors; signed-in results write
        \texttt{latticeTei.localAi} and appear on Federation standings.
  \item \textbf{Online human vs human:} Rated sectors (unassisted) write
        \texttt{latticeTei.online} via \texttt{reportLatticeOnlineMatch}
        when the game ends; casual and assisted sectors do not update TEI.
\end{itemize}
